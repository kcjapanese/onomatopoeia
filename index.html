WHY THIS WORKS

Not Another Word List

Most resources just give you definitions. That's not how language sticks.

Japanese is full of expressive sound words that textbooks barely cover. Words like ドキドキ, ピカピカ, モグモグ—they're everywhere in daily conversation, manga, and anime.

I learned English not through textbooks, but through what I call "illustration-based learning"—associating words with images and context until they became instinct.

That method worked because of something science has proven: the brain remembers images far better than text alone. It's called the Picture Superiority Effect, and research in Dual Coding Theory (Paivio, 1971) shows that when you process information through both visual and verbal channels simultaneously, you create two memory traces instead of one—dramatically improving recall.

This guide is built on that principle. Every word comes with an image, a sound, and a sentence—so your brain encodes it in multiple ways at once.
